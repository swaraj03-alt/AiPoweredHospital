Departments

Categorize doctors according to their medical specialty. This is essential for patient filtering, hospital management, or scheduling appointments in specialized areas.

  • Cardiology
  • Neurology
  • Hepatology
  • Pediatrics
  • Ophtalmology

Cardiology

The Cardiology department specializes in diagnosing, treating, and preventing diseases and disorders of the heart and blood vessels. Cardiologists manage conditions like coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and congenital heart defects.

Neurology

The Neurology department focuses on diagnosing, treating, and managing disorders of the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and nerves. Neurologists handle conditions such as stroke, epilepsy, migraine, multiple sclerosis, Parkinsonâ€™s disease, and neuropathies.

Hepatology

The Hepatology department specializes in the study, diagnosis, and treatment of liver, gallbladder, bile ducts, and pancreas disorders. Hepatologists focus on conditions affecting liver function, including hepatitis, cirrhosis, fatty liver disease, and liver cancer.

Pediatrics

The Pediatrics department specializes in the medical care of infants, children, and adolescents, focusing on their physical, emotional, and social health. Pediatricians diagnose, treat, and prevent a wide range of illnesses and conditions that affect children from birth to 18 years of age.

Ophtalmology

The Eye Care department, also known as Ophthalmology, focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of eye disorders and visual problems. Ophthalmologists provide medical and surgical care for patients of all ages to maintain healthy vision and manage eye diseases.
